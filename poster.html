A. A. Kirillov

Alexandre Alexandrovich Kirillov is one of the most influential mathematicians of the twentieth century. His orbit method — developed in the 1960s to classify irreducible unitary representations of nilpotent Lie groups — revolutionized representation theory and continues to shape modern mathematics and mathematical physics.

This conference brings together leading researchers to celebrate his 90th birthday and his profound contributions to representation theory, geometry, and mathematical physics.

In-person talks will be given on May 26–27. Zoom talks will be given in the following days (to be determined).
